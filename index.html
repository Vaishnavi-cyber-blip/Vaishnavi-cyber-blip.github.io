Hi, I’m Vaishnavi.

I work at the intersection of human–computer interaction, cognitive AI, and social computing. Growing up around people who are not tech-savvy, including my own family, made me aware of how wide the gap is between everyday users and the systems meant to support them. This gap is even sharper in India, where the pace of technological development often outstrips the ability of many communities to meaningfully engage with it. My experiences shape three directions I particularly want to pursue in my research: (1) building systems that take a ground-up approach, where technology adapts to real needs and constraints rather than expecting people to adapt to it; (2) designing transparent and reliable systems whose behavior aligns with human mental models; and (3) simplifying complex technologies so they are cognitively intuitive and can blend naturally into people’s lives, especially for users who are under-resourced, first-time digital users.

Projects and Experiments

Automated Site Suitability Validation for Irrigation Structures

Problem: Many irrigation structures in India are approved in hydrologically unsuitable locations, resulting in failed water capture and direct losses for smallholder farmers who rely on every intervention working.

Tool/Approach: Building an automated rule-based system that checks slope, catchment area, drainage distance, stream order, terrain, and LULC for every location and validate them.

Technologies: GEE, Python (Flask), GeoServer, QGIS

Progress: Completed proof-of-concept validating 30 sites; identified user errors, system errors, and rule violations. Working on algorithm.

3D AI Companion for Conversational Interaction

Problem: Most NLP-based AI systems feel static and monotonous, limiting engagement and making interactions feel transactional rather than human-like.

Tool/Approach: Built a real-time 3D avatar capable of voice responses, facial expressions, and dynamic reactions. Tried to connect it to an AI model to explore how visual embodiment and multimodal cues can make AI interactions more intuitive and emotionally grounded.

Technologies: React Three Fiber, Rhubarb Lip Sync, Blender.

Outcome: Developed a working prototype demonstrating expressive AI conversations, highlighting how embodiment can improve user engagement and reduce cognitive friction in human–AI interaction.

Trip Bharat — AI-Powered Travel Companion for India

Problem: Travel information in India is fragmented across blogs, forums, and informal sources, making it difficult for users to get reliable, personalized guidance for planning trips.

Tool/Approach: Built an AI-driven travel assistant that combines city insights, personalized itineraries, destination recommendations, real-time news, and weather updates into a unified experience. Designed modular backends for guiding, planning, and recommendation to provide context-aware answers and tailored travel suggestions.

Technologies: React, Flask, Crew Ai agents.

Outcome:Developed a functioning prototype that generates personalized itineraries, ranks destinations by user intent, provides city-specific insights, and surfaces live travel updates.

Consume Wisely — AI Product Label & Claim Verification

Problem:Consumers often rely on marketing claims that are unclear and exaggerated with actual product labels—especially in food and personal care products.

Tool/Approach: Built a system that extracts text from product labels uses AI to verify user-provided claims against the extracted label information, and fetches real-time product-related news. Designed modular endpoints for text extraction, claim analysis, and external article retrieval.

Technologies: Flask, Google Gemini, Tavily API, Python

Outcome:Developed a functional backend that evaluates claim accuracy, ingredients, and nutritional information while surfacing relevant news—supporting more transparent, evidence-based consumer decisions.

Firm Intelligence Bot — AI Document Query Assistant

Problem:Managers and analysts often struggle to extract actionable insights from long, unstructured firm-level documents, making information retrieval slow and error-prone.

Tool/Approach:Built an AI chatbot that allows users to upload documents and query them conversationally. Uses embeddings, vector retrieval, and conversational reasoning to surface relevant sections and answer questions with context awareness.

Technologies: Streamlit, LangChain, HuggingFace embeddings, ChromaDB, Groq LLMs.

Outcome:Delivered a functional prototype that supports multi-format documents (PDF/DOCX/TXT), preserves conversation context, and enables natural, interactive exploration of firm information.

Zoma — Multi-Agent Food Recommendation System

Problem:Food discovery platforms rarely adapt to a user’s context (weather, mood, diet, or location), making recommendations generic and impersonal.

Tool/Approach:Built a multi-agent system using CrewAI, where “Food Expert”, “Influencer”, and “Nutritionist” agents collaborate to generate context-aware dish recommendations. Integrated Travily Search for real-time data and Groq-powered Llama-3 for understanding nuanced user inputs.

Technologies: CrewAI, Llama-3 (Groq), Travily Search API, Streamlit.

Outcome:Delivered personalized dish suggestions based on weather, mood, diet, and location—demonstrating the potential of multi-agent reasoning in everyday decision support. Noted performance bottlenecks (~12–15 min/output), highlighting the need for optimized retrieval and caching.
