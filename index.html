Hi, I’m Vaishnavi.

I work at the intersection of human–computer interaction, cognitive AI, and social computing. Growing up around people who are not tech-savvy, including my own family, made me aware of how wide the gap is between everyday users and the systems meant to support them. This gap is even sharper in India, where the pace of technological development often outstrips the ability of many communities to meaningfully engage with it. My experiences shape three directions I particularly want to pursue in my research: (1) building systems that take a ground-up approach, where technology adapts to real needs and constraints rather than expecting people to adapt to it; (2) designing transparent and reliable systems whose behavior aligns with human mental models; and (3) simplifying complex technologies so they are cognitively intuitive and can blend naturally into people’s lives, especially for users who are under-resourced, first-time digital users.
